# new-ideas

## Purpose

Create a small set of candidate ideas, compare them against project strategy,
and move the strongest one into incubation.

## Inputs

- Product goal or market context.
- Current roadmap, TODO list, user pain, or opportunity notes.
- Known constraints such as budget, compliance, team size, and release window.

## Workflow

1. Scan existing work so new ideas avoid duplicating active efforts.
2. Generate two distinct ideas with audience, value, risk, and proof signals.
3. Compare the ideas with a direct tradeoff review.
4. Select one idea for incubation and record why the other idea lost.
5. Save enough context for `cook-idea` to challenge the winner.

## Verification

The selected idea must include target user, problem, proposed solution, expected
impact, risks, assumptions, and first proof step.

## Stop Conditions

- The project has no strategic or user context to evaluate ideas.
- Ideas would require credentials, legal approval, or production access that is
  not available.
- The top two candidates are too similar to compare meaningfully.
